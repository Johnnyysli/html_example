Fukuoka, Japan

please tag along

Kyushu, with its well-developed land, sea, and air transportation, is a region brimming with dynamic energy, cultural depth, and leisure activities. As Japan's third-largest island, it is renowned for its tonkotsu ramen, rejuvenating hot springs, diverse mountain ranges, and serene beaches. Fukuoka has become a global hub for startups, while the volcanic landscapes in the south attract attention with their eruptions and smoke. The Beppu Onsen area, famous for its hot springs, features eight steaming springs formed by geological activity, all set against a backdrop of stunning natural beauty. This geological activity also creates impressive peaks for hiking, such as Mount Kirishima. Kyushu beautifully combines modern innovation with a relaxed lifestyle, making it the perfect destination for those seeking a slower pace. Bangkok, Thailand

please tag along

The best time to visit Thailand is between November and March, when the weather is cool and dry. However, this period also coincides with peak tourist season, leading to higher prices for hotels and flights. Fall in Thailand tends to be wet, with September and October being the rainiest months in Bangkok. Despite the rain, you can still have an enjoyable experience! Traveling during the off-season has its perks, including significantly fewer crowds and the opportunity to benefit from low-season discounts, along with slightly cooler temperatures. As the monsoon season peaks in September and begins to ease in November, tourists flock in to enjoy the sunnier days. Traditionally, November marks the start of the busy season in Thailand, although it doesn't get particularly crowded until around Christmas.
